Labrador Retriever

Country of Origin: Canada

Retrievers are one of the most beloved and versatile dog breeds globally, known for their outgoing and gentle temperament. Originally bred in Newfoundland (now part of Canada) in the 19th century, Labradors were used by fishermen to retrieve fish and nets from icy waters. Their natural retrieving instincts and love for water quickly made them indispensable working dogs. Today, they excel in various roles, including service dogs, therapy dogs, search-and-rescue operations, and hunting companions.

Affectionate Nature: Gentle and people-oriented, thriving in family environments and forming strong bonds.

Trainability: Highly intelligent and eager to please, making them quick learners in obedience and advanced tasks.

Versatility: Excels in roles like therapy, service work, and outdoor activities such as swimming and retrieving.

Size: Medium to large

Personality: Outgoing, friendly, loyal

Life Expectancy: 10-12 years
